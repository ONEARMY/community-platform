## Capitalization

There are a few props to deal with text capitalization: `capitalize`, `uppercase`:

<Preview isColumn={true}>
  <Story name="Capitalized">
    <Text capitalize={true}>my content is all lowercase.</Text>
  </Story>
  <Story name="Uppercase">
    <Text uppercase={true}>my content is all lowercase.</Text>
  </Story>
</Preview>

## Sizes

There are a few props to deal with text sizes: `superSmall`, `small`, `medium`, `regular` and `large`:

<Preview isColumn={true}>
  <Story name="Super Small Text">
    <Text superSmall={true}>The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog.</Text>
  </Story>
  <Story name="Small Text">
    <Text small={true}>The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog.</Text>
  </Story>
  <Story name="Medium Text">
    <Text medium={true}>The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog.</Text>
  </Story>
  <Story name="Regular Text">
    <Text regular={true}>The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog.</Text>
  </Story>
  <Story name="Large Text">
    <Text large={true}>The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog.</Text>
  </Story>
</Preview>

Or you can set the prop `fontSize` yourself:

<Preview>
  <Story name="Font Size">
    <Text fontSize={30}>The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog.</Text>
  </Story>
</Preview>

## Pre-defined styles

You can configure `Text` components to use some pre-defined styles: `auxiliary`, `tags`, `paragraph` and/or `bold`:

<Preview isColumn={true}>
  <Story name="Auxiliary-style Text">
    <Text auxiliary={true}>The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog.</Text>
  </Story>
  <Story name="Tags-style Text">
    <Text tags={true}>The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog.</Text>
  </Story>
  <Story name="Paragraph-style Text">
    <Text paragraph={true}>The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog.</Text>
  </Story>
  <Story name="Bold Text">
    <Text bold={true}>The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog.</Text>
  </Story>
</Preview>
